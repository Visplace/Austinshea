Introduction

Where Contracts, Operations, and Financial Performance Meet

Austin Shea is a commercial real estate and operations professional with experience administering complex agreements, interpreting financial and legal obligations, managing lease portfolios, and supporting asset-level performance.

His work focuses on translating lease language and operating information into accurate financial outcomes. This includes base rent, CAM, real estate taxes, insurance, percentage rent, abatements, expense caps, recoveries, reconciliations, budgeting, and reporting.

Austin brings an analytical and finance-oriented perspective to commercial real estate. He is particularly effective at identifying discrepancies, organizing complex information, documenting conclusions, and coordinating across accounting, legal, ownership, leasing, vendors, and property operations.

Method

How I Approach the Work

1

Read the Source Documents

Important financial and operating decisions should trace back to the governing agreement, amendment, supporting record, or verified assumption.

2

Connect Language to Cash Flow

A lease provision is not merely legal language. It can directly affect revenue, recoveries, expenses, timing, risk, and asset value.

3

Document the Reasoning

Strong analysis should allow another stakeholder to understand the issue, the supporting information, and the conclusion reached.

4

Solve the Underlying Problem

The goal is not simply to identify a discrepancy. It is to determine what happened, what the agreement requires, and what action should follow.
